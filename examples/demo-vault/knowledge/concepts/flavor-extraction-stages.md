# Flavor Extraction Stages

Over the seconds of a shot, compounds dissolve in a rough order: acids and fruity notes first, then sugars and balanced body, then bitter and astringent compounds last. A well-pulled shot is stopped while it sits in the balanced middle — sour means under-extracted, bitter means over-extracted.

The stages `depend on` [[pre-infusion]], because an even, settled start determines whether the timeline progresses uniformly across the puck. The stages are `related_to` [[water-chemistry]], whose alkalinity shifts where the sour-to-bitter window sits, and the whole arc is what [[espresso-extraction]] is trying to land in balance.
