# Dose Ratio

Dose ratio expresses the relationship between the dry coffee dose and the liquid yield. A modern normale shot targets roughly 1:2 (e.g., 18g in, 36g out); a ristretto pulls shorter at around 1:1.5, and a lungo runs longer toward 1:3. The ratio is the lever a barista uses to move a shot along the intensity spectrum.

Dose ratio `depends_on` [[grind-size]], because the same ratio at a different grind extracts differently. It is `related_to` [[pump-pressure]], since the dose sets the bed depth and therefore the resistance the pump meets.
