# Pump Pressure

The 9-bar standard — roughly nine times atmospheric pressure — is the conventional target for espresso brew pressure, established by mid-century commercial machines. Pressure drives water through the coffee bed fast enough to emulsify oils and produce crema.

Pump pressure `supports` [[espresso-extraction]] as a necessary force, but it is not the whole story. The "pressure is everything" belief is `contradicted` by [[water-chemistry]], which argues that water composition limits extraction regardless of bar. Pressure is `related_to` both [[dose-ratio]] and [[pre-infusion]], since the resistance the pump works against is set by dose and how the puck is wetted first.
