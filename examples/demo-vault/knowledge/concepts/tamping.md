# Tamping

Tamping compresses the dry grounds into a level, uniform puck before extraction. The goal is less about absolute force than about consistency and a flat bed: uneven density lets water carve low-resistance paths (channeling) that extract unevenly.

Tamping `supports` [[espresso-extraction]] by setting up an even bed for water to pass through, and it is `related_to` [[grind-size]] because grind uniformity and tamp consistency together determine channeling. A pre-infusion school of thought `contradicts` the tamping-is-paramount view — see [[pre-infusion]], which argues gentle initial wetting forgives minor tamp imperfections.
