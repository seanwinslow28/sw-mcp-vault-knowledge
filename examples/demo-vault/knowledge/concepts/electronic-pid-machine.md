# Electronic PID Machine

A PID (proportional–integral–derivative) controlled machine holds brew-water temperature to within a fraction of a degree by continuously modulating the boiler heating element. Temperature stability is the variable it solves that earlier designs left to chance.

This design `supersedes` the [[lever-machine]] for commercial use, where shot-to-shot consistency across hundreds of pulls matters more than the romance of a hand-pulled profile. It is the direct descendant the [[lever-machine]] `evolved_into`. Its tight thermal control interacts with [[water-chemistry]] to make extraction repeatable.
