# Lever Machine

The spring-lever espresso machine, popularized in the 1940s, generates brew pressure mechanically: the operator pulls a lever that compresses a spring, and the spring's decompression drives water through the puck. Pressure is highest at the start of the pull and tapers as the spring relaxes — a natural declining pressure profile.

The lever design `evolved_into` the [[electronic-pid-machine]] as temperature control became the dominant concern. For commercial high-volume settings the [[electronic-pid-machine]] has largely `superseded` it, though levers retain a devoted following for the profile their [[pump-pressure]] curve produces.
