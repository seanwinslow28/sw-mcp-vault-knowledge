# Water Chemistry

Water is more than 98% of a shot of espresso, and its mineral content governs how much flavor it can dissolve out of the coffee. Total dissolved solids, general hardness, and alkalinity (the buffer that resists pH change) all shape extraction. Too soft and the shot tastes flat; too hard and it scales the machine and mutes acidity.

Water chemistry `supports` [[espresso-extraction]] as a foundational variable. It directly `contradicts` the [[pump-pressure]]-alone school of thought — pressure cannot extract what the water cannot dissolve. It is `related_to` [[flavor-extraction-stages]], since alkalinity especially shapes where the sour-to-bitter timeline lands.
