# Pre-Infusion

Pre-infusion wets the coffee bed at low pressure before the pump ramps to full force. The brief soak lets the puck swell and settle evenly, reducing the chance that the sudden onset of 9 bars blasts a channel through a weak spot.

Pre-infusion `supports` the [[flavor-extraction-stages]] by promoting an even start to extraction. It is `related_to` [[pump-pressure]], since pre-infusion is defined precisely by holding pressure low before the main ramp. The pre-infusion-forgives-tamping argument `contradicts` the view that [[tamping]] precision is paramount — gentle wetting mitigates some of what an imperfect tamp would otherwise cost.
